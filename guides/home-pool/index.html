Home & Water Safety (Layers of Protection)

In Arkansas, many pediatric drowning deaths occur in open water. Prevention works best when families use multiple layers: barriers, supervision, and readiness.

Layer 1: Active supervision

  • Designate a “water watcher” (one adult, eyes on water)
  • No phone, no scrolling, no “just for a second”
  • With young children: stay within arm’s reach in and around water

Context: CDC notes drowning is a leading cause of death for young children. Source.

Layer 2: Barriers & environment

  • Fence pools/spas and use self-latching gates when possible
  • Empty buckets/tubs immediately after use
  • Use toilet lid locks for toddlers
  • After bath: drain the tub right away

In-home drowning hazards and “small depth” risk are emphasized in CPSC safety materials. Source.

Layer 3: Life jackets & equipment

  • Use U.S. Coast Guard–approved life jackets in open water
  • Keep a phone nearby for emergencies
  • Know the address/location you’re at (especially at lakes/rivers)

Layer 4: Skills & response readiness

  • Learn CPR (infant and child)
  • Consider swim skills when age-appropriate
  • After any submersion event: seek medical evaluation

National burden and morbidity context: CDC drowning facts (PDF).

Arkansas data note: ICDR reporting for deaths occurring in 2023 shows most pediatric drowning deaths occurred in open water, with a smaller fraction in pool-or-bathtub settings. Source.
